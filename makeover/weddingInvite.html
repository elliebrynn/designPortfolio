Wedding Invitation

Stationary Design

The invitations I created for my July 2017 wedding was a hands-on project from start to finish. I had a very specific vision in mind for a unique, modern wedding suite for my husband and I, so I took it upon myself to see out each and every detail. Throughout the wedding I chose to incorperate clear, acrylic elements (invitations, seating chart and table numbers, etc.).

The typographic design is created using Butler Stencil, a strong serif typeface with a graphic twist. The letterforms' stencil-like negative spaces are reminiscent of the minimal philosphy of removing what is unnecessary. It is paired with a sturdy sans serif, League Spartan Regular, that balances the weight of the headline. The clear substrate emboldens the type in a way that is minimal and refined.

The front of the invitation is printed in black ink on transparency sheets and the second piece is printed by THikit Printing on a super heavyweight stock with black edge coloring to encourage tactile interaction. The clear sheet is stacked on backside of the thick stock, as pictured, and the printed colorblocking frames the type from below. When the clear sheet is lifted, the black type casts a shadow on the stock beneath it, further demonstrating the dimensionality of the pairing. The wedding details are printed on the back of the thick stock. Both pieces are encased in a dark grey envelope that I hand addressed with a loose brush script.
