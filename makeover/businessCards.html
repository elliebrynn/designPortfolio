Business Cards

Graphic Design & Letterpress

I created these business cards for myself during the letterpress course at IU. The class was challenged to create something personal using techniques and skills we had learned that semester. I wanted to create an exercise for myself that would help me learn to communicate my personal brand, and create something the might potentially be useful in my upcoming job search.

I set out by listing personality traits and characteristics that define who I am and set me apart. What I'm good at, what I'm passionate about, and how I aspire to live should dictate how I visually represent myself. I am a team player with a lighthearted demenor and positive attitude. I am a designer who cares deeply about others and the impact my work has on them. I want to create with a goal of social betterment.

Because of these insights, I determined that my visual brand identity should reflect lightheartedness and compassion. For this reason, I chose a variety of colored papers that are slightly desaturated. The color palette communicates playfulness in a way that is equally rooted and stable. I created a wordmark for myself that hints at the upturning shape of a cartoon smile. The bold glyphs that make up the typeface I chose are softened by the slightly rounded terminals they are formed with, lending them to a friendly delivery. I also created a confetti like texture using the uppercase "I" to further communicate a spirit of whimsy.

I designed the file in Illustrator for relief printing, then sent the file off to Boxcar Press, who created the photopolymer plate. I set the plate in a lockup on the press and printed on the multicolored cardstock from French Paper.
