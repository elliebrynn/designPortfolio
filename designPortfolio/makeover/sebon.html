Seb'On French Food Truck Bistro

Identity Design Case Study

The challenge was to develop a brand identity for a food truck concept, designing a producing many of the necessary artifacts for the Production for the Graphic Designer course at Indiana University. Final deliverables included the logo, menu, take-out packaging, gift card and sleeve, website design, external truck design mockup, and cookbook.

The concept driving this brand identity is about breaking the stereotype that the French are judgmental and snobby, to creating a light-hearted and welcoming environment for free expression with a rich and colorful meal. The intention is to bridge the gap between the French culture, and a funky and colorful style. The connection is not forced because Henri Matisse’s paved the way with his freeform line drawings and bright cutouts. His iconic work was the source of much inspiration for this vision.

For each individual project, my process was to conduct research, write a creative brief and create a visual moodboard before I began designing. There were generally have 2 weeks from when each project was introduced to the final critique. Each project was then evaluated based on meeting the project objective, effectiveness of visual communication, production accuracy, craft/quality of submitted piece, effort, accurately following submission guidelines, critique presentation, critique preparation, and critique participation.

Throughout the development of this cumulative project, I learned skills and concepts required to produce a comprehensive brand identity for a potential client. I am able to not only design hypothetical digital elements, but I have learned to process through the conceptual aspect of brand development and can now potentially transition to helping consult a real business through this process. I have also learned to become acutely aware of the details for every aspect of each project. It is so easy to let things slip through the cracks if you are not informed about the production process! I am so grateful for each of these intense projects. It was so growing for me to carry an initial concept and vision through many pieces of collateral.
